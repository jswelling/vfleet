Volume Rendering Concepts

Data volumes are used as input to a transfer function, which produces a sample volume of colors and opacities as output. That volume is rendered to produce a final image.

  • In practice, the data volumes are usually grids of numerical data.
  • Often, there is only one data volume, and the transfer function is a simple table lookup.
  • Regular x-y-z data grids are easiest and fastest to handle, but algorithms exist for handling irregular grids like finite element models.
  • Geometrical primitives can be handled by "rasterizing" them into data grids.
